  • Powerful automation capabilities for secure Identity Governance

    Identity governance and administration (IGA) is a policy framework that helps manage and secure an organization's digital identities. AD360 provides custom automation policies to simplify labor-intensive tasks such as user provisioning, password resets, group membership modifications, and more, all without human intervention. AD360 also helps enforce control over administrative activities by implementing approval-based workflows for error-free management of identities. The focal point of IGA is to comply with various regulatory standards and AD360 helps your organization comply with the GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, FISMA, and ISO using its built-in compliance reports, adaptive authentication, ML-based analytics, and SIEM integrations.

  • Automated access controls for effortless access management

    Access management (AM) focuses on securing identities to ensure that the right users have access to the right resources, at the right time. With AD360's adaptive MFA, users can securely access organizational resources from any place, at any time. Different access control policies will automatically apply to users based on various factors such as location, IP address, device, and time of access. Users can also leverage the MFA-secured SSO to safely access multiple applications with a single identity, without the hassle of multiple logons. AD360's AM capabilities also include entitlement management, UBA-based audit reports, password management, and business workflows.

  • Role-based security for privilege management and Zero Trust

    Zero Trust works on the principle of "never trust, always verify" to secure organizations. A Zero Trust environment, when tightly integrated with privileged access management, enforces the principle of least privilege in the organization. With AD360, an admin can create granular roles and assign them to users so that they can perform administrative tasks as technicians, without elevating their permissions in the native environment. Admins can also implement a multi-level approval workflow to scrutinize technician activities before a task is executed. AD360's Zero Trust capabilities include role-based access control, adaptive authentication, identity analytics, business workflows, and controlled automation.
